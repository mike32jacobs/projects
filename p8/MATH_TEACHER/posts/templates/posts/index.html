Welcome to Math Teacher Problems

Math teachers have all sorts of problems. They range from dealing with a general population which suffers from math-phobia, to an intense unacknowledged jealousy of English teachers. There are many more, but this website does not to talk about all of them.

Symbols

Displaying Math Symbols

Displaying mathematical symbols and notation will be be a much easier endeavor if math teachers can add a few tools to their tool kit. In particular, it will be important to understand LaTex, Mathml, and mathjax. each of these can lead any math teacher down a rabbit hole for which they don't have time. This section will briefly explain these three tools, and provide a few resources for those who want to learn more.

Issues

Problems (issues)

A major problem that math teachers face is how to display mathematical notation in distinct digital environments. No matter how experienced you are, you are sure to encounter problems in displaying math symbols. You might solve the problem for Google docs, only to discover that your solution won't work in Google Slides. You may think you have solved a problem, but discover that it is only solved for some browsers.

Problems (Question Banks)

Eventually, this site will be a place where teachers can go to access crowd-sourced question banks. Users will contribute their own questions, and they will also helped check the work of other users. Eventually, these question banks will give teachers the ability to easily assign different types of problems to their students. The page is currently under contruction. Check back to see this feature at an unspecified date in the future.
