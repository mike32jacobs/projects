Math symbols don't always display as expected

Any teacher who needs to use images of symbols throughout the content of of their lessons has likely encountered an issue with symbols. Some math equation editors are better than others. Undoubtedly, math teachers have experienced the frustration of trying to make some math equation or image appear how they want it. One example is the brackets which are used to wrap a system of equations.

There are a few possible solutions to this problem, but each has it's costs.

  • Use LaTex to generate the equations. There are a number of equation editors available on the web. Not all of them have all of the features a teacher might need. Editing laTex is a skill that math teachers should add to their toolbox.
  • Use mathml in your html code. It is a markup language designed for use with mathematical languge. The goal of MathML is to "enable mathematics to be served, received, and processed on the World Wide Web, just as HTML has enabled this functionality for text."
  • Use images. You can take a screenshot of the system of equations. This will work, but if you do it frequently, it could be a time consuming process. A look at Problem 2 will show you how to take a screenshot, and then upload a desription into the Canvas LMS. Making the image accessible is important, but also time-consuming
  • Use Mathjax to do this for you. This is a very effective solution, but it requires a basic knowledge of html, and javascript. Althouth the solution is effective, it is not reasonable to expect that all math teachers will be willing and able to add this to their tool box.
